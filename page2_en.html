Semester objectives

In DUT Computer Science, the semester abroad can take place in the 4th semester. This semester has random objectives depending on the students participating in this project, we can retain some major goals:

  • Develop your language culture, especially English.
  • Improve your motivation and thus succeed in your semester.
  • Expand your CV and be better seen by future employers.

Bilateral Convention

What is a Bilateral Convention ?

A bilateral convention is an agreement signed between academic institutions from different countries that allows students to perform one or more semesters in an establishment abroad.

UQAC

The establishment in Chicoutimi is the University of Quebec at Chicoutimi (UQAC)

Prerequisites : French

Mobility period : January - April

Program

The professional profile aims to promote the progression of the student in his organization or his insertion into the job market. It allows the student to improve our knowledge to propose solutions to specific problems of the Informatic field, to develop a capacity for synthesis, a rigor and a critical sense in a field in constant evolution and develop communication skills.

The program totals 45 credits. It is composed of a schooling from 24 to 30 credits in informatic, from 0 to 9 credits among the following programs:

  • Master in Project Management

  • Masters in Business Administration

  • Master of Management in Organizations

The student completes his program by one of the activities of 15 credits following: the internship, the test or the intervention project.

UQAM

Prerequisites : French

Mobility period : January - April

Program

The Master's program in Computer Science aims to initiate the computer research. It's a research program that allows developing self-employment skills in the context of work supervised by the research director. Potential candidates are strongly encouraged to contact the professors of the computer science department to find a director and this,even before applying. The master's degree in computer science aims as much to prepare its graduates for the labor market and for doctoral studies.

Specific objectives:

  • Familiarization with scientific publications and communications

  • Development of autonomy, critical sense and ability to work in groups

  • Acquisition of new knowledge

  • Development of a spirit of synthesis and the ability to consider original and practical solutions in practice.
