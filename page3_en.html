Identity documents and visas

Check the required travel documents (national identity card, passport, visa) for entry and stay in your country of destination at the embassy and consulate of that country in France. With regard to the passport, some countries require a minimum validity.

Within the European Union (EU), a valid national identity card is sufficient for traveling. Outside the EU, most states require a valid passport several months after the expected date of return to France. In due time, contact your prefecture for possible renewal.

Remember to keep photocopies of the documents you take with you (in case of loss or theft abroad) and to bring you at least 2 passport photos.

In case of loss or theft of the passport or the national identity card during a stay abroad, you must, first of all, make the declaration to the local police authorities. From this declaration, the embassy or consulate of France may establish, as appropriate, an emergency pass or passport. Attention this formality is paying and requires a delay.

Minor children

A minor child traveling alone or with his parents must be provided (depending on the country of destination):

  • its national identity card (especially for EU countries and the Schengen area) or
  • his passport or
  • his visa passport

With regard to the documents required by the country of destination, it is recommended to consult the embassy or consulate of the country of destination, on the one hand, and the corresponding "Travel advice" sheet, on the other hand.

In addition, some countries may require proof that the other parent authorizes this trip, in the form of a simple free paper mail (which is not issued at the town hall).

If a visa is needed, contact the foreign consular services in France in due time, as it may be necessary to have sufficient time to produce certain documents depending on the country (form, photograph, criminal record, bank statement , return transport ticket, etc.).

Animals

In general, do not plan to go abroad with your pet in an impromptu way: you will first have to inquire at the embassy of the country of destination several weeks or even months in advance. Ask what are the papers to present at customs, the required vaccinations and the necessary health certificates, knowing that certain inoculations are required up to 6 months in advance. Know that some countries prevent the entry of animals on their territory and others require an import permit.

Money

Check that you have sufficient means of payment (cash, traveler's checks, credit card ...) to cover your living expenses and your return.

The amount of withdrawals by credit card is limited to a certain amount per week. Check with your bank before departure.

For any entry or exit of France sums, securities or value of an amount equal to or greater than 10,000 euros, you must establish a prior declaration to the customs office.

Automobile

Find out about local rules of the road.

If you use a motor vehicle, do you have the following documents:

  • Gray card
  • International insurance card (find out about its validity depending on the country where you go)
  • International driving license (to be requested from the prefecture or sub-prefecture of his place of residence)
  • Logbook (issued exclusively by automobile clubs), if required
